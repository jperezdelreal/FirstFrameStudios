## Deliverables

**Document:** `games/ashfall/docs/SPRITE-ART-VISION.md`

**Sections:**
1. **Intensity Escalation Strategy** — VFX overlay and stage art handle Round 1→3 escalation, NOT sprite variants. Keeps sprite count manageable, allows emotional consistency, focuses generation on baseline emotion.
2. **Art Style Target** — HD pixel art with cel-shading (Last Blade 2 / Guilty Gear XX / Garou quality). 64×64 px base provides facial expression detail and body language clarity. Avoids 3D, photorealism, or chibi aesthetics.
3. **Character Movement Personality Framework** — Kael: "Economy of Motion" (minimal wind-ups, snaps to position, martial artist discipline). Rhena: "Explosive Commitment" (big wind-ups, wide arcs, street brawler momentum). Movement energy descriptors guide FLUX creative direction.
4. **Emotion Scale Matrix** — Detailed emotion descriptions for every action state (idle, walk, all attacks, block, hit, KO, win) for both characters. Kael NEVER loses composure (even peak intensity = control). Rhena is ALWAYS intense (even idle = ready to explode).
5. **Non-Negotiable Consistency Rules** — Pixel-exact proportions (Kael 12px shoulders, Rhena 14px), identity markers (Kael's ponytail, Rhena's burn scars always present), ember colors (Kael = blue, Rhena = orange, never swap).

**Key Decisions:**
- Intensity escalation via VFX, not sprite variants (3× sprite reduction, faster generation)
- Art style: HD pixel art + cel-shading (readable emotion, stylized consistency)
- Personality through movement (economy vs. explosion) guides FLUX beyond technical specs
- Emotion matrix prevents "generic fighting game face" syndrome
- Identity markers and proportions locked in prompts to prevent AI drift
