# Ashfall Closure Ceremony — 2026-03-10T1842Z

## Session Summary

**Project:** Ashfall (1v1 fighting game, Godot 4)  
**Event:** Full-team closure retrospective  
**Outcome:** Comprehensive retrospective delivered; project officially shelved  

## What Happened

Full team (Solo, Yoda, Chewie, Ackbar, Mace) conducted a structured closure ceremony for Ashfall after 2 complete sprints. Each discipline provided a comprehensive retrospective:

- **Solo** analyzed architecture patterns, system design, and recommendations for scaling the Lead Architect role
- **Yoda** examined vision, design decisions, and fighting game genre challenges for AI-only teams
- **Chewie** assessed Godot 4 capabilities, engine gaps, and AI agent integration challenges
- **Ackbar** reviewed quality metrics, testing strategy, and QA gaps
- **Mace** analyzed production metrics, team velocity, capacity, and process effectiveness

## Key Takeaway

**Fighting games are fundamentally misaligned with AI-only development.** The genre requires subjective feel-tuning, hitbox intuition, and frame data interpretation that AI agents cannot reliably deliver. Studio pivoting to simpler, faster-to-ship genres.

## Critical Lessons

1. Genre choice is the primary lever for AI team viability
2. Art pipeline validation must happen pre-production
3. Automated integration gates needed from Day 1
4. Lead Architect role must split at 10+ agents
5. Design reviews for feel-dependent mechanics are critical

## Decision Made

Ashfall is officially shelved. Team consensus achieved.
